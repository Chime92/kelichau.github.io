    Artist & Lifelong Learner

    Kelly Chau is a Vietnamese-American artist based in Atlanta, Georgia. She is currently an upcoming senior at Georgia Gwinnett College with a major in psychology. At a young age, Kelly showed passion in the arts including photography, singing, acting, and any other creative form of self-expression. Other than her passion for the arts, she places high importance on mental and physical health and often shares her ideas on prevention over curation.

    Besides being a full-time student, she also runs a freelance photography business. She had the opportunity to work with GGC's Office of Public Affairs as an assistant photographer, shooting various events on campus. She lives by her motto, "Learning to be comfortable with being uncomfortable," and believes that one should always create their own path in life.

    Realizing that most people believe in the limits of circumstance, she would respond to the question, "What do you plan to be after graduating?" with variations of "I don't know," or "Psychologist". The answer that she now shares is that she wants to live in the moment and continue to be happy and healthy, welcoming life with wide open arms---a concept that most still do not understand, but one that makes her eager and hopeful to give insight in others. She believes that although the future may be unpredictable, one thing that human beings will always have control over is their perception of life.
