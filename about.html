    Artist & Lifelong Learner

    Kelly Chau is a Vietnamese-American artist based in Atlanta, Georgia. She is currently an upcoming senior at Georgia Gwinnett College studying psychology. At a young age, Kelly showed interest in the arts including photography, singing, acting, and other creative forms of self-expression. Other than her passion for the arts, she places high importance on mental and physical health and often shares her ideas on prevention over curation.

    In 2014, she decided to launch a small photography service for clients while continuing to improve her skills. In 2015, she had the opportunity to work with GGC's Office of Public Affairs as an assistant photographer. She currently works as a part-time photographer for Portrait Innovations while finishing up her last year of undergraduate studies.

    Realizing that most people believe in the limits of circumstance, she would respond to the question, "What do you plan to be after graduating?" with variations of "I don't know," or "Psychologist". The answer that she now shares is that she plans to welcome life with wide open arms—a concept that most still do not understand, but one that makes her eager and hopeful to give insight in others. She lives by her motto, "Learning to be comfortable with being uncomfortable," and strongly believes that one should always create their own path in life rather than waiting for it to be paved for them.
