Photography Scam Jobs

Photographers beware of these fake job offers.

Posted by Keli Chau on October 23, 2018

I have received this kind of email before and though I never bothered responding to them, I thought I could help add SEO by writing a blog post about it for other photographers.

The people operating these scams would find photographers from LinkedIn, Indeed or other similar sites with a listed email address. They would then email you with job details and tell you that they will send you a check to deposit into your bank account, before you've even worked, and instruct you to forward any remaining balance to other "workers" on the project (hair stylist, florist, etc.) before the check bounces.

In the email I received this time, the business is called "Moment Capture Studios". The job they described is for an assistant photographer on a 3-day production shoot, with a $900 pay and travel expenses covered. The address I found on their cookie-cutter website leads to a law firm.

What to look out for:
1. Grammar and spelling mistakes
2. If they ask for your full name, mailing address, phone number
3. They’ll tell you that they’ll send you a check via USPS
4. Check their website URL and the photos and address listed on there
5. They’ll tell you your pay
6. They give you all these details before you’ve even spoken to them
